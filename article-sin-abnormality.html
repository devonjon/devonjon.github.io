The Abnormality of Sin

Published on May 5, 2025 | Theological Ethics

Therefore, just as sin entered the world through one man, and death through sin, and in this way death came to all people, because all sinned.

Romans 5:12

We have a tendency as humans to try and justify ourselves, our friends, our loved ones and even strangers. We use phrases like, "He's only human" - which is true, we are only human. The issue is that that doesn't excuse us from sin, but in fact re-affirms our sin. We justify ourselves by excusing ourselves based on our frailty. We normalise sin, and we normalise sinful behaviour. Whereas God teaches us that sin is abnormal and holiness is normal. Sin is not relatively worse than holiness, and holiness is not relatively better than sin. Instead, holiness is held as a complete distinction from sin. We are not merely defective, we are dead. Sin is death, and holiness is life. Sin kills, holiness brings life.

✝

Jesus did not die on the cross to raise us from one level of humanity to another, but to raise us from death into life. He did not die to make us eternally abnormal, but to bring us back to eternal normality. While we live on earth, we are perceived as abnormal only because we are citizens of another kingdom, under the rule of another king. So let us live as though we are, and though the world may call us abnormal, let us never call sin normal or justify the evil intentions of our hearts.

"For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord." — Romans 6:23

Let us rather go to Jesus as humble beneficiaries of justification and mourn over our sin and long for His holiness.
